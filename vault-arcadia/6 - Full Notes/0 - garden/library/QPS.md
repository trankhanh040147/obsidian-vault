**Queries per second** (**QPS**) is a measure of the amount of search traffic an [information-retrieval](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_retrieval "Information retrieval") system, such as a [search engine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Search_engine "Search engine") or a [database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database "Database"), receives in one second.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queries_per_second#cite_note-1) The term is used more broadly for any [request–response](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Request%E2%80%93response "Request–response") system, where it can more correctly be called [requests per second](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requests_per_second "Requests per second") (RPS).
